<h1>digraph</h1> <h2 class="modsummary">Directed Graphs</h2> <div class="description"> <p>The <code>digraph</code> module implements a version of labeled directed graphs. What makes the graphs implemented here non-proper directed graphs is that multiple edges between vertices are allowed. However, the customary definition of directed graphs will be used in the text that follows. </p> <p>A <span id="digraph"> </span><em>directed graph</em> (or just "digraph") is a pair (V,&nbsp;E) of a finite set V of <span id="vertex"> </span><em>vertices</em> and a finite set E of <span id="edge"> </span><em>directed edges</em> (or just "edges"). The set of edges E is a subset of V&nbsp;×&nbsp;V (the Cartesian product of V with itself). In this module, V is allowed to be empty; the so obtained unique digraph is called the <span id="empty_digraph"> </span><em>empty digraph</em>. Both vertices and edges are represented by unique Erlang terms. </p> <p>Digraphs can be annotated with additional information. Such information may be attached to the vertices and to the edges of the digraph. A digraph which has been annotated is called a <em>labeled digraph</em>, and the information attached to a vertex or an edge is called a <span id="label"> </span> <em>label</em>. Labels are Erlang terms. </p> <p>An edge e&nbsp;=&nbsp;(v,&nbsp;w) is said to <span id="emanate"> </span><em>emanate</em> from vertex v and to be <span id="incident"> </span><em>incident</em> on vertex w. The <span id="out_degree"> </span><em>out-degree</em> of a vertex is the number of edges emanating from that vertex. The <span id="in_degree"> </span><em>in-degree</em> of a vertex is the number of edges incident on that vertex. If there is an edge emanating from v and incident on w, then w is said to be an <span id="out_neighbour"> </span> <em>out-neighbour</em> of v, and v is said to be an <span id="in_neighbour"> </span><em>in-neighbour</em> of w. A <span id="path"> </span><em>path</em> P from v[1] to v[k] in a digraph (V,&nbsp;E) is a non-empty sequence v[1],&nbsp;v[2],&nbsp;...,&nbsp;v[k] of vertices in V such that there is an edge (v[i],v[i+1]) in E for 1&nbsp;&lt;=&nbsp;i&nbsp;&lt;&nbsp;k. The <span id="length"> </span><em>length</em> of the path P is k-1. P is <span id="simple_path"> </span><em>simple</em> if all vertices are distinct, except that the first and the last vertices may be the same. P is a <span id="cycle"> </span><em>cycle</em> if the length of P is not zero and v[1] = v[k]. A <span id="loop"> </span><em>loop</em> is a cycle of length one. A <span id="simple_cycle"> </span><em>simple cycle</em> is a path that is both a cycle and simple. An <span id="acyclic_digraph"> </span><em>acyclic digraph</em> is a digraph that has no cycles. </p> </div> <datatypes> <datatype> <name name="d_type" /> </datatype> <datatype> <name name="d_cyclicity" /> </datatype> <datatype> <name name="d_protection" /> </datatype> <datatype> <div class="description"><p>A digraph as returned by <code>new/0,1</code>.</p></div> </datatype> <datatype> </datatype> <datatype> <name name="label" /> </datatype> <datatype> </datatype> </datatypes> <div class="functions"><h4>Functions</h4><hr /> <div class="function"> <h3 id="add_edge/3">add_edge/3</h3> <h3 id="add_edge/4">add_edge/4</h3> <h3 id="add_edge/5">add_edge/5</h3> <type name="add_edge_err_rsn" /> <div class="description"> <p><code>add_edge/5</code> creates (or modifies) the edge <code><anno>E</anno></code> of the digraph <code><anno>G</anno></code>, using <code><anno>Label</anno></code> as the (new) <a href="#label" class="seealso">label</a> of the edge. Returns <code><anno>E</anno></code>. </p> <p><code>add_edge(<anno>G</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>V1</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>V2</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>Label</anno>)</code> is equivalent to <code>add_edge(<anno>G</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>E</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>V1</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>V2</anno>,&nbsp;<anno>Label</anno>)</code>, where <code><anno>E</anno></code> is a created edge.
